Oral Surgery

  • Home
  • Oral Surgery

Oral surgery entails surgical operations on various oral structures such as teeth, gums, and jaws. Tooth extractions, dental implants, gum grafts, and corrective jaw surgery are examples of these operations. Oral surgery is often performed by oral and maxillofacial surgeons or periodontists who have received advanced training in these procedures.

Benefits

By addressing concerns such as tooth decay, gum disease, and biting abnormalities, whole-mouth rehabilitation can improve oral health and avoid tooth loss.

Oral surgery seeks to enhance the general health and function of the mouth by correcting problems with the teeth, gums, and jaw joints.

Oral health disorders, if left untreated, can cause pain, discomfort, and trouble eating or speaking. These symptoms can be relieved and your quality of life improved with oral surgery.

To identify the best course of action for your unique oral health needs, speak with a certified oral surgeon or periodontist. They can assess your circumstance and make the best recommendations.
